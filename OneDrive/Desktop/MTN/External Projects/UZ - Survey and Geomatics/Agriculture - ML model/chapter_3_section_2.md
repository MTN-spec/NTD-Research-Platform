# 3.2 Research Design and Philosophy

This section establishes the research philosophy and design that guided the study. The research is grounded in the positivist paradigm which prioritizes objective observation and measurement. Positivism assumes that reality exists independently of the researcher and can be quantified through empirical data. This philosophical stance is appropriate because the study relies on numerical values from electronic sensors. Saunders et al. (2019) state that a positivist approach allows for the discovery of causal relationships through statistical analysis. The study aims to find predictable patterns between spectral reflectance and moisture content without subjective interpretation.

A deductive research approach was adopted to test existing theories about remote sensing. The study starts with the established principle that water content in soil affects the electromagnetic energy reflected from a surface. It then applies this theory to the specific context of maize production in the Chinhoyi district. Creswell and Creswell (2018) describe deduction as a top-down process where hypotheses are formulated and then tested against real-world observations. This approach ensures that the development of the machine learning models remains rooted in scientific principles.

The research design is quantitative and applied in its nature. It is quantitative because it uses statistical metrics for evaluation. The design is also applied as it seeks to solve the practical problem of irrigation management for farmers. A quasi-experimental method was used to compare the performance of the automated alert system against traditional scheduling. This comparison provides evidence of the operational benefits of satellite-informed decision making. The study uses a quasi-experimental design because it compares different irrigation strategies in a real-world setting. This method allows for the assessment of the system's impact on agricultural productivity. Such an approach provides practical insights that are valuable for the farming community in Mashonaland West.
